About me

I affect policy change by coordinating advocacy, communications, and marketing.

Audiences have different motivations and knowledge. To affect change, you must know your audience, adapt your message, and talk to them in language they understand. I want to help organizations do that.

For the past 18 years, I have built a broad, strong foundation of experience in public policy, communications, business, and government. Combining that experience with tenacity and innovation, I can help you affect change through communications.

  • Know
  • Adapt
  • Communicate
You have to know what motivates your audience. Different people will be motivated to take action for different reasons. You start by understanding their motivations.
You must develop custom messaging and have the tools and resources to communicate them to the different audiences. This could be as broad as targeted advertising to as precise as tools that adjust materials on the fly.
It is a rare person that takes extra time to understand what someone is trying to say, so you have to communicate at a level they quickly understand. Speak to what they know with words that are easily understood. Breaking out every term you used in business school or sounding like a walking thesaurus won't help you.
